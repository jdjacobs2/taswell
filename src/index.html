WINDSONG is a superb three stateroom, center cockpit sailing yacht in enviable condition, maintained by her owner in turnkey, fully equipped ocean ready cruising condition. She is a totally custom-built blue-water sailing yacht that can be easily handled from the cockpit and cruised by one couple. With a keel depth of 5.5 ft and a mast height of 63 ft WINDSONG easily navigates the ICW.

WINDSONG was a fresh-water boat for her first ten years. During their ten years the second owner sailed the U.S east coast and the Bahamas. The third and present owner also covered the U.S. east coast and the entire Caribbean, often 2 or more times.  WINDSONG has always been non-smoking and no-pets.

WINDSONG has been continuously upgraded, including virtually all mechanical, rigging and electronic systems.  Qualified mechanics have diligently professionally performed under owner's close supversion all necessary and optional maintenance and upgrades.

Why Buy This Boat?

Taiwan's reknowned boat builder Ta Shing and famed British designer/architect Bill Dixon endowned Windsong with highest quality materials and workmanship. A quick inspection reveals its beauty: its teak interior, its spacious salon and cabins, its roomy heads with stall showers, its cedar lined lockers, its padded vinyl easily removable overhead liner. and its statin varnished thick teak and spruce cabin sole, to name just a few. Its thick solid hand layed fiberglass hull, sound insulated engine compartment, its through-bolted stainless steel backed deck hardware, in-mast wire conduits, among many other features, insure a safe, sturdy, easily maintainable boat in all conditions throughout the world.

Beginning forward is a V-berth guest cabin with full-sized berth. Behind and to port is the second guest cabin with two over-under single berths. Opposite is a spacious guest head with separate shower stall.

Proceeding aft Windsong boasts its unusually luxurious main salon. Outfitted as is the entire boat in the finest teak with exceptional craftmanship, it is spacious, airy. bright and confortable. Aft of the main salon to starboard is the U-shaped galley. Opposite to port, on the other side of the companionway is the fully equipped navigation station.

Aft of the navigation station, to starboard, is the engine room and passageway to the master stateroom. The master stateroom highlights a centerline queen berth and a mirrored vanity. To starboard is the large en-suite master head with separate shower.

Forward "V" Berth

The generously sized forward cabin has a full-width berth, upholstered ultra-suede settee to starboard, mirrored vanity and storage cabinet, roomy cedar-lined hanging locker, five spacious drawers and two storage lockers. The cabin is well lit with two overhead dome lights and two brass cabin lights; the cabin is also well ventilated with an large overhead hatch, two ports and two dorade vents. Access to the chain locker is in this cabin.
Port Guest Cabin
The port guest cabin features upper and lower single berths, a generous cedar-lined hanging locker, storage locker, recessed bookshelves and a dressing table with a lifting top, a mirror above and three drawers beneath. The cabin is lit with overhead dome and brass cabin lights and is also well ventilated with an overhead hatch, a port and a dorade vent.
Guest Head and Shower
The guest head services both guest cabins and can be entered directly from the forward cabin (for privacy) or from the forward passageway. The head is well sized with a separate shower stall, teak seat and teak floor grate. Ample storage is provided in three large storage cabinets, and a sliding three panel vanity. The counter-top is edged with teak fiddle trim and surrounds a mirror-finished stainless steel sink. A large mirror hangs on the forward wall.  The Raritan toilet has a separate 30-gallon holding tank. An overhead hatch, a port and a dorade vent, provide ventilation.
Master Stateroom
The aft owner’s stateroom is a large, airy, very accommodating cabin. There is a comfortable centerline island queen-sized berth and two ports at the transom providing continuous air circulation. Two curved ultra-suede settees are positioned on either side of the berth and face a dressing table with an opening table top, a teak trimmed mirror above and swing-out vanity stool below. In addition to a large overhead hatch, three additional ports and two dorade vents ventilate the cabin. Lighting includes four brass cabin lights, a large fluorescent light and three overhead LED dome lights. Storage space is extremely generous in this cabin featuring almost 6' of cedar-lined hanging lockers, a Formica-lined wet gear locker, four large drawers (one with security lock). Two storage lockers plus ample storage under the settees complete the picture.

Owner's Head and Shower
The en-suite owner's head features a separate shower stall with a clear Plexiglas door and teak grate. A built-in automatic dryer is mounted outboard of the shower behind a waterproof vinyl cover. There are also large storage compartments and a roomy linen closet. The counter-top is edged with teak fiddle trim and surrounds a mirror-finished stainless steel sink. Above, a teak-framed mirror hides a medicine cabinet. The Raritan head has a separate 30-gallon holding tank. Two ports ventilate the head; two dome lights and a fluorescent light provide evening illumination.
Main Salon
The main salon is very bright and open with two large overhead hatches and four fixed hull ports set above recessed bookshelves and four long fixed trunk cabin windows. To port is a large oval-shaped bolstered settee upholstered in luxurious fabric that surrounds a formal hi-lo oval teak table (seats eight for dinner). This large table can be removed (it has its own custom-built storage locker) to reveal a small coffee table that can be lowered for informal gatherings, cocktails or coffee. On the starboard side, two comfortable teak and upholstered swivel club chairs sit either side of a curved teak table. When inverted, the top of this table provided a secure setting for drinks, inside is ample storage for wines and liquors.  Two forward outboard cabinets hide stereo speakers. WINDSONG also has a handsome curved teak mast cover that conceals the keel-stepped mast. The companionway has a clear Plexiglas sliding hatch above that allows light to enter the salon and it can be closed using three solid teak boards or two screened teak boards. All are stored in a custom-designed compartment under the steps.
Galley

A seagoing, U-shaped galley is located starboard at the base of the companionway. This location allows the cook to work without interfering with traffic. Teak louvered cabinets above the counters with larger cabinets below the counters including a large two-shelved circular cabinet with automatic light, eight drawers, a large under counter pot & pan bin, a cabinet below the stove for large cooking utensils and stow-away stainless steel garbage bin. The Frigoboat DC keel cooled refrigerator has both top and front loading doors.  A totally independent separate keeled cooled Frigoboat DC freezer has its own dedicated digital thermostat and temperature gauge.

There are deep stainless steel double sinks with hot and cold pressure water, a microwave oven, Sea Ward 3-burner stove with oven and broiler, teak towel dispenser, dual teak spice racks, and back-up fresh and salt water foot pumps. A deck prism mounted above the stove provides natural daylight and four undercounter LED’s, one dome LED, and a LED brass cabin light provide artificial illumination.

Electrical
The generator room access doors are located in the passageway leading to the aft cabin giving virtually 360 access to the 8kw Onan MDKD generator.  The generator has run less than 2500 hours.  A sound-damping hush box surrounds the generator. Like the entire vessel the generator has had constant care. All equipment is bonded to ship's copper plate grounding system plus grounding line from mast to engine block.

400 watt hours of solar panels provide additional power to the 8 100 amp hour AGM house batteries and one group 8D AGM engine battery (all new 2015).

Another source of energy is the Magna 2800 watt inverter charge that has a remote control and gauge at the navigation station.

Navigation Electronics

The navigation station is located to port off the companionway stairs, faces forward, and has ample chart-sized teak table.  Navigation and other instrumentation is displayed on panels above and to the side of the navigation table.  Additional storage is provided above the instrument panel, in two cabinets below the chart table, and in a bookcase inset into the aft bulkhead. A deck prism, chart table light, fluorescent light and overhead LED dome light provide ample illumination. New instrumentation was installed in 2015, including a Garmin 7608 and 7612 touchscreen chartplotters at the navigation station and on deck respectively, 3 Garmin digital displays, a Garmin VHF, a ICOM 803 SSB, a Pactor Dragon P4 7800 modem, and a Garmin autopilot.  The chart plotters, the autopilot, the VHF, bow thrusters and the anchor windlass all include wireless remote controls.

Sails and Rigging

Mounted on the cockpit coming are two electric variable speed Andersen 62 ST mainsheet winches,  two electric variable speed Andersen 40 ST jib sheet winches, and an Andersen low profile traveler winch.  Mast mounted are a Lewmar 43 STC main halyard winch, a Lewmar 40 STC genoa halyard winch, and a Lewmar 30 STC Staysail halyard winch.  The main sail has in mast roller furling that either of the starboard Andersen electric winches can furl.  Both jibs have new 2014 Schaffer stainless steel roller furlers.  All standing rigging is new 2014.  Spare halyards include one main and two spinnaker halyards.  Included are a well maintained 130 genoa, a 100 jib, a in-mast roller furling main, and a large gennaker with sock.

Mechanical

A Yanmar 77 hp turbo diesel model 4JH-DTE, with less than 5200 running hours, has been expertly maintained and looks like new.  Recent upgrades include a new turbocharger in 2014 and new starter in 2003. The engine is easily serviced through six removable engine access doors located in the passageway from the salon to the aft cabin and in the rear head. The engine compartment is well insulated to control noise.

A 14 gallon per hour Spectra Cape Horn watermaker is split between the engine compartment and underneath the banquet in the main salon.   An Atwood 10.5 gallon water heater, 110 volt & engine driven provides hot water throughout the vessel, including to  the transom shower.

There also are three CruisAir self contained reverse cycle air conditioners, a 12,000 BTU unit in the main salon, one 8,000 BTU unit that cools the two forward cabins, and one 8,000 BTU in the aft owner’s cabin.  Each unit has it own digitally controlled thermostat to cool, dehumidify, or heat.   The mechanicals furthermore include a three blade feathering Max Pro propeller and Maxwell vertical windlass VWC-3500.

Deck, Hull and Cockpit

This is a Bill Dixon designed yacht with low-profile line, extended sugar-scoop transom, Awlgripped blue hull with white accent stripes, teak decks, and meticulously varnished teak toe-rail. With her 15' wide beam, deep forefoot and short overhangs, she has all the below-deck room on would want in a yacht of this size.

Two 2016 coats of epoxy barrier coat and two coats of hard Petit blue paint applied to bottom. Hull is insulated with P/U foam insulation in living areas. The hull exterior is protected teak rub rails with stainless steel strake plates.

The hull also includes S/S stanchions with double lifelines, six S/S cowl vents mounted on fiberglass dorade boxes, six S/S mooring cleats and teak grab rails.  Roomy lazarettes at the aft corners furnish additional storage for line, spare anchors, fenders, hoses, electrical cords, etc.

The cockpit includes a custom pedestal with a 32” diameter teal trimmed steering wheel.  A custom removable legless teak table provides a comfortable cockpit dining area and stows securely against the pedestal.  Custom vinyl covered cushions comfort the cockpit benches.

Most importantly the cockpit floor is covered with easily removable teak cockpit grates to help insure that sand and dirt does not reach Windsong’s interior.  Two cockpit speakers provide music from the stereo at the navigation station.

A stainsteel Garhauer motor hoist at the transom provides easily removability of the outboard from the dinghy and mounting on the rail mounted teak motor mounts.   Removable screens protect the six Lewmar deck hatches.

Deck canvas (color toast) includes a bimini under the solar panels, a connector with strata-glass between the hard dodger and bimini, cockpit side panels with strata-glass, sunbrella cockpit sunshades, wheel/pedestal cover, deck hatch covers, and toe rail covers.

The cockpit also has a full complement of navigation equipment.  A Garmen 7612 chart plotter, two Garmin digital displays, a Garmin autopilot display and control, Garmin wireless autopilot remote control, Lewmar wireless anchor windlass/bow thruster remote control,  and Garmin remote wireless VHF handset, are all mounted in the cockpit.

A stainless steel folding ladder that stows against the rear transom bulkhead lowers to provide access from the swim platform into the water.  The swim platform additionally has a stowable shower head and hose that provides both hot and cold water.  A second folding stainless ladder stows against the lifelines and removably mounts amidships to either of the vessel’s sides to provide easy access to either a dinghy or the water. The transom ladder provides the advantage of transom steps, yet keeps the vessel's length under 50 feet, permitting lower dockage costs and easier maneuverability.

Safety

Windsong has a full complement of safety equipment.  Included among these are DSB Deutshce Schlauchboot GmbH 6 man life raft in a rigid container, a Switlik MOB-8A man overboard inflatable module, 2 ACR 496 EPIRBS, 2 Res-Q-Link personal locator beacons, six fire extinguishers, 7 life jackets, 2 jack lines, 2 safety harnesses, and a emergency life sling.  A Harken bosun's chair and an ATN mast climber round out the emergency equipment.
